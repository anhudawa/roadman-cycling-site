# Sunday — Weekend ride check-in

Write the lightest prompt of the week. Ask what the group rode and what they took from it. Warm, short, one hook.

Good raw material for the hook:

- Weather (heat, wind, rain, fresh snow on the tops).
- Big race weekend — mention the race only if one is actually happening that weekend. Don't invent.
- A specific route type — hilly, flat, gravel, commute, indoor session that had to replace a ride.
- A small lesson — "I rode without a gel and bonked at 90 min" kind of thing.

Structure:

1. One-sentence hook about the weekend or the weather or what you noticed out there.
2. Two-part ask: "What did you ride, and what did you learn?"
3. Sign-off: `— Ted`

Keep to 40–80 words. This one should feel the closest to a friend asking a question at the cafe stop.

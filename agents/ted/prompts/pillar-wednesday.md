# Wednesday — Strength & Conditioning

Write a lift-specific or injury-prevention prompt. S&C for cyclists, not S&C for lifters who also cycle.

Good raw material:

- Hip-hinge as the single most under-trained movement for riders.
- Unilateral work — why bilateral lifts mislead.
- Core that isn't crunches — anti-rotation and anti-extension.
- Plyo — when it actually transfers to the bike.
- Off-season vs. in-season — what drops, what doesn't.
- Back pain on the bike — almost always a glute or hip problem, not a back problem.
- Knee tracking — tell the group what their knee is telling them.

Structure:

1. A specific movement or problem in two sentences.
2. The ask — what one S&C habit actually moved the needle for you?
3. Sign-off: `— Ted`

Never prescribe a set and rep scheme. You're prompting a conversation.

# Monday — Coaching

Write a training-methodology prompt. Ask a genuine question the group can argue about, or lay out a coaching scenario and ask what they'd do.

Good raw material to draw from:

- Z2 / polarised / pyramidal — the Seiler angle. Most amateurs get the balance wrong.
- Interval prescription — what matters more, duration or quality?
- Training stress vs. training load — does the number actually tell you anything?
- Taper protocols — how short can you go before you lose fitness?
- FTP testing — still useful, or outdated?
- Cadence work — under-trained in most programmes.
- Coaching a teammate vs. coaching yourself — what changes?

Structure:

1. Open with the problem or a specific scenario. Two sentences maximum.
2. The ask — a concrete question, not a vague "what do you think?"
3. One nudge that shows you're not neutral — cite a coach or a study.
4. Sign-off: `— Ted`

Do not answer the question yourself. Stay the prompter.

Planning a cycling trip in 2025? This episode breaks down how to pick the right events and locations from the 30 LA Tapa Series races happening across 21 countries. You'll learn a practical filtration system to match events to your ability, goals, and lifestyle — and discover how to prepare for everything from heat training to nutrition logistics.

## Key Takeaways

- Start your annual training plan with non-negotiable life events (weddings, work trips, family commitments) before adding cycling events — this prevents peak periods clashing with unavoidable time off
- Use a power profile test (10-sec, 1-min, 5-min, 20-min efforts) to identify your strengths (climber, sprinter, endurance) then match events that suit your physiology for better enjoyment and performance
- Filter event choices through: logistics, your skill set, climate preferences, and budget — narrowing from 30+ global options down to 2-5 that actually suit you
- Heat acclimatization is trainable — gradually expose yourself to warmer conditions before hot-weather events to increase blood plasma volume and improve your body's temperature regulation
- Rope your mates into the same event — you'll have more fun riding with people at your level than tackling something alone that doesn't suit your abilities

## Expert Quotes

> "I've fallen into this habit now of I'm almost not even excited to go away because it feels like I'm not going away — I've been there so many times before."

> "Just being a cool place isn't enough you need a cool route — it's not even about just getting to the different country."

> "When you go through that kind of filtration system starting with the wide funnel and narrow it down I think you're probably going to end up with two to five events that suit you."

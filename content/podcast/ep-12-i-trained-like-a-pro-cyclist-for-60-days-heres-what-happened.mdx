Anthony went from 468W to just under 500W on the 3-minute critical power test in 60 days. He dropped 8kg. He was coached by Alex Camier from Team Decathlon and Ian Field, who coached Matteo Trentin to the European championship. This is what actually happened, and it wasn't what he expected.

## Key Takeaways

The numbers moved fast under a reverse periodization structure. Intensity first, then volume. Two to three key sessions per week hitting exact power targets, longer weekend rides to build durability. 12-minute power went from 370W to over 415W. 8kg gone. Not through calorie restriction, but through fueling properly and staying consistent across the full 60 days. The training load maxed out at 20 hours a week, which sounds manageable until you're actually living inside it.

Here's the thing Anthony kept coming back to: the training wasn't the hard part. The hard part was the life required to support the training. Airports, late edits, meal prep that became a second job, solo hours on the bike while everyone else was getting on with their lives. Pros aren't superhuman. Their lives are just built around the training. Everything else gets organized around that. If you want results like this as a self-coached rider with a real life, that organizational piece is what you're actually solving for, not the interval sessions.

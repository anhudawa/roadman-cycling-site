One of the most honest conversations Anthony has ever had on the podcast. Lachlan Morton — EF Education pro, Alt Tour pioneer, and one of cycling's most fascinating minds — talks about why the World Tour wasn't enough.

## Key Takeaways

- Morton's Alt Tour (riding the entire Tour de France route solo, unsupported) changed his relationship with cycling
- The peloton rewards conformity — adventure racing rewards curiosity
- His evolution as an athlete came from asking "why am I doing this?"
- The line between professional and personal has blurred in the best way
- Community and connection matter more than results

## Expert Quotes

"I got to a point where I'd won races, I'd been in breakaways at Grand Tours, and I still felt empty. The Alt Tour was the first time on a bike where I felt completely alive."

"When you take away the race radio, the team car, the schedule — and it's just you and the road — you find out very quickly what you actually love about cycling."

In this episode, Anthony reflects on over 10 years of cycling knowledge, sharing the most valuable lessons he’s learned from riding and speaking to experts. From changes in his views on nutrition and training to insights gained through real-life experience, Anthony distills a decade of cycling into under 5 minutes. Here’s what he’s learned:

Periodization: Anthony emphasizes the importance of structuring training into phases—base, build, peak, and recovery. It’s not about hammering efforts every day, but about progressive development towards specific goals.

Recovery is Key: Rest days are just as crucial as hard training days. Overtraining can lead to burnout, so Anthony encourages cyclists to listen to their bodies.

Consistency Over Intensity: Long-term success in cycling comes from consistency. It's the seemingly small, steady efforts that contribute to the biggest gains, not just the intense training blocks.

Bike Handling: Anthony highlights the importance of mastering bike handling skills. Whether it’s moving up in a group ride or cornering at speed, proficiency on the bike is key to success.

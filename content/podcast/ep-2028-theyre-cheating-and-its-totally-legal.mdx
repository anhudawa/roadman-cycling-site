Forget doping—pro cycling's most impactful unfair advantage is hiding in plain sight. This video exposes the truth about TV motorbikes and team cars create powerful slipstreams that change races like the Tour de France, often determining stage wins. 

From Tim Wellens' 2025 victory (where Quinn Simmons was very vocal) to infamous cases involving Pogačar and Roglič, we dive into the science, history, and politics of motor drafting. With insights from wind tunnel studies, rider testimonies, and expert opinions, we explore how camera bikes can tip the scales—and why the current rules are failing.

## Key Takeaways

- TV motorbikes and team cars create powerful slipstreams that can give riders a significant drafting advantage — wind tunnel data shows this effect is measurable and race-altering, not just speculation
- The Tim Wellens 2025 stage win controversy (with Quinn Simmons publicly protesting) highlights how motor drafting can directly determine race outcomes in Grand Tours
- Current UCI regulations on vehicle proximity are vague and inconsistently enforced, meaning the same "legal cheating" keeps happening race after race
- Cases involving Pogačar and Roglič demonstrate that even the biggest names in cycling benefit from — or are disadvantaged by — motor drafting decisions outside their control
- Until the UCI introduces strict, measurable distance rules and real-time enforcement technology, motor drafting will remain professional cycling's most impactful grey area

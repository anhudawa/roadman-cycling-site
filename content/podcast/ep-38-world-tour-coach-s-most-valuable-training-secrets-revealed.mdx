Steven Barrett coaches Felix Gal and the AG2R Decathlon World Tour squad. He came into the studio and we spent two hours going through what pro cyclists actually do, what the data misses, and why the most scientifically perfect training plan you've ever written is worth nothing if the rider doesn't trust you.

## Key Takeaways

Barrett made a point that stuck with me: FTP, VO2 max, critical power, all of it gets talked about constantly, but bunch efficiency barely gets measured. How much energy is a rider spending relative to everyone else doing the same work in the peloton? AG2R flagged some of their guys as physiological standouts who are bleeding watts just sitting in the bunch. Valentine Rauzel at 54 kilos isn't surviving crosswinds and positioning fights at Quickstep on raw watts per kilo. He doesn't waste unnecessary energy. That's a trainable thing, but most programs don't even track it.

The other thing Barrett said that I keep coming back to: consistency beats perfect periodisation if the athlete doesn't believe in the coach. He's had the most validated, periodised training blocks sit completely unused because the rider thought he was an idiot. A worse plan with full buy-in outperforms it every time. That's not a nice thought for anyone who's spent hours building the perfect training schedule. It means trust comes first, the science comes second.

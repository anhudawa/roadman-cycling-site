## Key Takeaways

- Sharing prize money and bonuses with your entire lead-out train isn't just generous—it's a tactical motivator that builds loyalty and ensures your team delivers when it counts most.
- Modern cycling professionalism is a double-edged sword: young riders now have access to better training science and resources than ever, but the psychological cost of constant optimization can burn out careers faster.
- There's a meaningful difference between aggressive sprinting and reckless sprinting; calculating risk and knowing when to brake separates champions from casualties, especially on dangerous descents like Tour of Catalonia.
- Even after winning 158 races, respect from management can evaporate overnight—Greipel's contract offer was slashed to one-third salary after recovering from injury, a brutal reminder of cycling's ruthlessness.
- Helmet safety innovation lags because marketing prioritizes aesthetics over protection; riders and teams need to demand better equipment standards instead of accepting poor-quality gear from sponsors.

What if everything you think you know about fuelling is slowing you down or even hurting your health?

In this episode, world tour nutritionist Dr. Sam Impey joins us to dismantle outdated advice—and reveal how elite cyclists are now pushing up to 120g of carbs per hour without crashing or bloating. But is this a good thing for amateur cyclists to do and what are the pitfalls of high dose carbs? 

From the science of gut training to the myths around weight loss, recovery drinks, and carb loading, Sam explains why more carbs + smarter timing = less burnout and better performance.

We cover what most amateurs get wrong, how to fuel for different training loads, and how AI tech is helping riders like Tom Pidcock personalize nutrition in real time.

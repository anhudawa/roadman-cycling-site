Dr. Mike Ormsby, professor of nutrition and physiology at Florida State University, has spent two decades studying what happens when you eat protein before bed. The answer breaks something most cyclists have been told since they started racing.

## Key Takeaways

Ormsby's lab tested this directly. Thirty to forty grams of protein, two hours after dinner and within thirty minutes of bed, added 150-200 calories to total daily intake. Body fat didn't move. What did move was resting metabolic rate the next morning and muscle protein synthesis overnight. A 12-week resistance training study showed the pre-bed protein group came out with larger muscle cross-sectional area, better one-rep max strength, and increased lean mass compared to the group who had nothing. If you trained in the evening, 80-85% of studies showed even better outcomes when you paired that with bedtime protein. The mechanism isn't magic. Your gut digests and absorbs amino acids while you sleep. Ormsby's team confirmed this in the early 2010s, alongside Luke Vanlon's lab, when almost nobody else was doing this work.

The cycling culture piece is worth naming here because it's specific and it's wrong. I've had this conversation with NDY members more times than I can count. Riders obsessing over race weight, coaches telling athletes to fast overnight, the idea that anything eaten after 8pm is going straight to fat. I raced for a French team where the directeurs treated evening eating like a moral failing. That fear comes from a world where lighter always means faster, which isn't always true, and definitely isn't true when you're under-recovered and losing muscle you need to hold power. Ormsby is clear that total daily protein is the primary lever. Pre-bed timing is secondary. But if you've been skipping a protein hit before sleep because you're worried about body composition, that's the habit worth revisiting. Keep carbohydrates under 10-15 grams in the shake. Don't make it a full mixed meal. And if it disrupts your sleep, stop, because sleep is where all of this actually works.

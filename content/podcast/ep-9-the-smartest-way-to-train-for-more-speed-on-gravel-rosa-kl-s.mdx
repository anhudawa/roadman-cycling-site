Rosa Klöser's VO2max block is nine days total. Not four weeks, not six weeks. Nine days, two high-intensity sessions back to back, one recovery day, repeat that three or four times, done. I've had NDY members grinding away at threshold work for months thinking that's how you build top-end speed. It's not. And the tire thing she mentions will probably annoy a few people who've been running 35mm because they think narrower equals faster.

## Key Takeaways

Klöser trains polarized. 80% zone 2 and fatmax intervals, 20% high intensity. But her V2 max block is shorter than most coaches would prescribe. Two days on, one day off, repeated three to four times, nine days total. Day one is four sets of eight minutes at 106% FTP, around 320 to 325 watts at her size. Day two goes shorter and harder, three sets of ten 40/20 second intervals above 120% FTP. That's it. Block over. Then volume. Her coach uses lactate testing to set those percentages, not a 20-minute FTP test, which means the numbers are actually accurate rather than a guess based on a guess.

On nutrition, she plans 10 to 20 grams of carbs per hour above whatever she can currently handle, and she tests that ceiling during long training rides, not on race day. On tires, she maxes out her tire clearance, 45mm on her Canyon Grail, and her rolling resistance data from Silverstone testing shows the 2 to 6 watt cost of a bigger tire gets paid back in 45 seconds or more on a single technical descent if you can carry speed through corners. If you're on 35mm because you think it's faster, run the maths on that.

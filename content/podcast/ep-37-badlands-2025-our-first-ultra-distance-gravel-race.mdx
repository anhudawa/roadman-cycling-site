## Key Takeaways

The training approach here wasn't threshold blocks and FTP tests. It was aerobic efficiency and decoupling — watching heart rate to power ratio improve over months of riding. Sarah came in pretty untrained, which helped, but the gains are real. She won her age group at the Rift. The coaching was also about deciding what not to train for. There's a sprint section on the Saturday club ride where everyone goes full gas. Sarah has nothing there. That's correct. That's the job done right.

The taper is 10 days, which is long. Short hard spins, 90 minutes to 2 hours, no duration. The thinking is that the taper length is proportionate to the event length and the buildup. On gearing, they settled on 48/35 front with 10/36 rear. The logic is simple: if a climb is steep enough that you can't get up it on a 35/36, it's going to cost you more energy to ride it than to walk it. With 20% pitches in the Badlands parcours, some sections are just a walk. Build the gear ratio around what you'll actually ride.

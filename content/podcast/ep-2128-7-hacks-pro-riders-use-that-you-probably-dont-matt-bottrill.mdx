We're deconstructing the whole art of time trialing—from bike and wheel setup, to kit, pacing strategy, and body position—with someone who's spent years obsessively perfecting every marginal gain.

## Key Takeaways

- 85% of time trial performance comes from the athlete's body position, not the bike or kit—master your position first, then optimize everything else around it
- Pacing strategy changes with wind direction: push 10-15 watts harder into headwinds using a medium position, then utilize your aerodynamic position in tailwinds while staying on top of your gearing
- Wheel and tire interaction matters more than individual components—modern rims are 25-27mm internally and pair best with 26mm tires; matching the right tire to your rim shape is where real gains happen
- Don't obsess over drivetrain marginal gains (chains, pulleys, pedal stack height)—wheels make the biggest difference to rolling resistance, but the real art is learning to feel how your bike handles and adjusting your pacing accordingly
- Use real-world data to find your actual CDA (drag coefficient) during races, not just in the wind tunnel—test different pacing strategies in your local time trials where wind patterns are predictable

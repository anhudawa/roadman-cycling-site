faq:
  - question: How often should I get a bike fit?
    answer: >-
      Dr Andrew Pruitt recommends that recreational riders over forty get a bike
      fit reassessment at least once a year. The body changes with age, training
      load, and injury history, so fit is an ongoing process rather than a
      one-time event. Pruitt advises working with a fitter who knows your
      history so adjustments can be made in context.
  - question: What causes hand numbness when cycling?
    answer: >-
      According to Dr Andrew Pruitt, the most common cause of hand numbness on
      the bike is too much weight bearing down through the hands. This typically
      points to a position that is too stretched out or a saddle that is tilted
      forward, shifting load onto the arms. Correcting saddle angle and
      handlebar reach usually resolves the problem.
  - question: Does bike fit actually make you faster?
    answer: >-
      Dr Andrew Pruitt argues that comfort and speed are inseparable, meaning a
      properly fitted position directly improves performance. Discomfort forces
      the body to compensate, which wastes energy and reduces power output. A
      fit that allows you to hold an aerodynamic position without pain will
      always be faster than one that prioritises aerodynamics alone.
  - question: Can I get a bike fit using my smartphone?
    answer: >-
      Dr Andrew Pruitt predicts that smartphone-based automated fitting apps
      will significantly reshape the bike fitting profession in the coming
      years. These tools use video and motion analysis to suggest positional
      changes without needing a specialist present. However, for riders with
      injury histories or complex biomechanical needs, an experienced fitter
      remains the more reliable option.

## Key Takeaways

- Bike fit is an ongoing process, not one-and-done: get a professional assessment once yearly, especially as your body changes with age and flexibility decreases
- A thorough pre-fit physical exam (flexibility, foot mechanics, hip motion, spinal alignment) is what separates great fitters from average ones who only read screen numbers
- Hand numbness in cycling typically comes from too much weight on the bars combined with poor core strength—raise your bars, strengthen your core, and upgrade bar material and padding
- Comfort and speed are inseparable: an aerodynamic position you can't hold is worthless; test positions in the lab, physiology lab, track, then road before finalizing
- Automated bike fit apps are coming but currently miss the front-view (Z-plane) data needed to assess knee alignment, hip rock, and foot dynamics—human interpretation remains crucial
- Elite riders look beautiful on bikes because of genetic gifts and self-selection, not because that's the formula for others; make the bike fit the rider, not the rider fit the bike

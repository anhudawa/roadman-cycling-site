Cycling used to be about how you rode, not how you looked. Then came Pas Normal Studios — a brand that flipped that equation and, for a moment, defined what it meant to be cool on two wheels. But somewhere between €400 jerseys, gravel wins, and fashion week runways, the ride changed. 

In this episode, we trace the rise and fall of cycling’s most divisive brand — from cult status to parody, from road cred to runway. It's a story about aesthetics, identity, and what happens when image outpaces intent. Whether you ride in silence or style, this one asks a simple question: what does it really mean to belong in the bunch?

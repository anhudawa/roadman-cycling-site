Pas Normal Studios started as 50 sets of kit made for one race in the Alps. Within 18 months they had a waiting list and a cult following that made every other jersey on the road look like shite. This is the story of how a Copenhagen fashion designer accidentally rewrote cycling's dress code, tried to crack the World Tour, and then quietly walked away from the sport entirely.

## Key Takeaways

Carl Oscar Olsen, co-founder of Woodwood, commissioned that first run of kit because he couldn't find anything that didn't look like a NASCAR reject. Clean lines, Italian-made, no logos. He wasn't trying to build a brand. But riders at Lamar tried to buy the kit off his back, and that was that. The name came from Tyler Hamilton's book The Secret Race — 'pas normal' was peloton slang in the EPO era for a rider going suspiciously well. I've had Tyler on the podcast and he talked about exactly this. Olsen took that phrase and flipped it. Brand built on irony from day one.

The Mechanism Pro line was legitimately good kit, €350 jerseys and €400-plus bibs, and they made a push for a World Tour team deal in 2022-2023 that went nowhere. So they went to gravel, built PA Racing properly with support cars and mechanics, and Carol Minion won both Traka and Unbound in 2024, so the kit clearly worked. Then Moncler money came in through Archive SRL, and that was the end of it as a cycling brand. Pitti Uomo in January 2025, 30 looks, five of them cycling kit. Fashion editors front row. Nobody from the cycling press in sight. They haven't gone anywhere. They've just stopped pretending to be a cycling brand.

Pas Normal Studios was founded in Copenhagen in 2014 by Carl Oscar Olsen, a fashion designer and co-founder of Scandinavian label Woodwood, and Peter Lang, a business strategist. The brand name derives from French cycling slang documented in Tyler Hamilton and Daniel Coyle's 2012 book The Secret Race, where 'pas normal' (literally 'not normal') was used by riders to describe suspiciously strong performances in the EPO era. The founders commissioned an initial run of 50 jersey and short sets for the L'Étape du Tour, a grandfondo finishing atop Alpe d'Huez with approximately 5,000 metres of elevation, and built an 18-month waiting list without a formal marketing campaign. Their Mechanism Pro line, launched circa 2022, was priced at over €350 for jerseys and over €400 for bib shorts, and was developed with bonded seams, proprietary Italian fabrics, and wind tunnel-tested aero profiling. Negotiations for a World Tour team supply deal in 2022-2023 failed to reach agreement. Pas Normal Racing, the brand's gravel team, recorded wins at both Traka and Unbound Gravel in 2024 with rider Carol Minion. In 2024, Archive SRL, a Milan-based investment firm connected to the Moncler-backing Ruffini family, acquired a significant stake in the brand. At Pitti Uomo in January 2025, Pas Normal presented 30 looks of which only five were cycling kit, signalling a full pivot toward lifestyle fashion.

Cam Phipps won Unbound 2025 in 8:37 averaging 295 watts raw and 194 grams of carbs per hour. Alex Wild ran 296 normalized and 260 raw for the full 200 miles, finished 12th-13th, and is still in the Lifetime Grand Prix top 10. Here's what actually happened out there.

## Key Takeaways

The race was decided early. Cam and Simon got into the break before Divide Road, avoided every surge effort the chase group absorbed, and rode consistent endurance-plus-tempo all day. Meanwhile the chase group swelled to 80 riders after Little Egypt when people stopped working, split hard in the crosswinds, and never got organised. Alex's read: getting into that break costs you maybe 30-60 seconds on the technical sections, then the group sits up and you get it back without burning a threshold match.

Two punctures cost Alex the race. First was a tread puncture, one minute lost, plugged and absorbed into the next group. Second was a pinch flat on the front in the crosswind section, three to four minutes gone finding the bead and sorting bent bars, and that was the last group on the road. He finished solo TT from there at 22.1 average. His tyre call, 50mm Maxxis Tracers, was wrong. Ian Boswell ran 45mm Pathfinders and had no punctures. Alex says he'd go 45 Pathfinders or 22mm AirTracks next time, not the Tracers whose tread is too thin for Kansas rocks.

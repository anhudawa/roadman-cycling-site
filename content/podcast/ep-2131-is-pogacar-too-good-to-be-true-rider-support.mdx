  - question: 'Why do pro cyclists all wear the same time trial helmet in team time trials?'
    answer: >-
      World Tour teams are contractually required to use their title sponsor's
      equipment in races, which means all riders wear an identical helmet
      regardless of whether it is the fastest option for each individual.
      Personal aero optimisation takes a back seat to sponsor compliance in
      team events. This is why you will not see riders switching to a
      different brand's helmet even if wind tunnel data might suggest it would
      be quicker for them specifically.
  - question: 'How do I set accurate heart rate training zones for cycling?'
    answer: >-
      The most reliable method is to base your zones on a properly conducted
      maximum heart rate test or a lactate threshold field test, rather than
      using age-predicted formulas. Once you have an accurate threshold heart
      rate, you can divide your zones proportionally around that figure.
      Consistency in how you test matters more than which exact zone model you
      follow.

- Set your heart rate zones using a 25-minute threshold test (68% of your max effort HR = Zone 1, 69-83% = Zone 2) rather than relying on max heart rate percentages alone

Cycling has a strange obsession — and it’s not carbon frames or VO2 max. It’s shaved legs. 

For over a century, cyclists have ritualistically removed leg hair, often without really questioning why. The industry says it's about aerodynamics. A 2014 wind tunnel study claimed it could save you 79 seconds over 40km. But some experts now argue we’ve misunderstood the science. 

In this video, we explore where the tradition came from, what the data really says, and why leg shaving might be less about speed — and more about identity. From tribal signals on group rides to the science behind drag, this is the deeper story behind cycling’s most enduring ritual.

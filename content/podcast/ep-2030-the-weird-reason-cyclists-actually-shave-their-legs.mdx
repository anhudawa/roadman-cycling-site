## Key Takeaways

The Specialized Morgan Hill wind tunnel study tested triathlete Jesse Thomas at 30 miles per hour, shaved versus unshaved. The result: 15 watts saved, 7% reduction in drag, 79 seconds over 40 km. They ran the same test on five more riders using what they called the Chewbacca scale, rating leg hair from 1 to 10. Every single rider showed improvement. Hairier legs, bigger time savings. That should be the end of it. Except Burroughs pointed out in the 1930s researchers found that a rough sphere produced half the wake of a smooth one, the same principle behind golf ball dimples. And Primoz Roglic won the 2023 Vuelta with hairy legs. And nobody shaves their arms, despite arms accounting for 35 to 40% of the surface area of your legs.

When British Cycling surveyed its athletes on why they shave, aerodynamics didn't make the top three. Massage came first — pros get up to 25 massages a month and hair pulls. Wound care came second. Tradition came third. Alex House, former EF Education pro, told Bison magazine he started shaving at 14, calls it a right of passage, and only shaves when he's racing. That's the honest answer. It's not about drag coefficients. It's about the signal your legs send at the start of a group ride when nobody knows who you are yet.

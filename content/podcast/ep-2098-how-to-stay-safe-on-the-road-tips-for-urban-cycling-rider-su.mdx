Urban cycling can feel like navigating an action movie, but it doesn't have to be terrifying. Anthony and Sarah break down practical, confidence-building strategies for staying safe around traffic, intersections, and unpredictable road users—plus they tackle whether altitude tents and expensive gear upgrades are actually worth your money.

## Key Takeaways

- Scan the road constantly for hazards like tram tracks, wet leaves, and road markings; they're the leading cause of crashes in urban environments
- Take the full lane defensively when needed—especially near parked cars (to avoid dooring) or when a safe passing distance isn't possible; your safety overrides traffic flow
- Make eye contact with drivers at intersections to confirm they've seen you; it's a small habit that significantly reduces accidents
- Avoid lingering in the blind spots of buses and trucks; anticipate what other road users will do rather than assuming predictable behavior
- Altitude tents compromise sleep quality and require 8+ hours daily use for weeks to show results; unless you're chasing marginal gains or training for high-altitude events, easier training methods deliver better ROI
- The 80/20 training principle works regardless of how you structure the 20% intensity—what matters most is respecting your body's recovery needs and tracking how you feel alongside the data

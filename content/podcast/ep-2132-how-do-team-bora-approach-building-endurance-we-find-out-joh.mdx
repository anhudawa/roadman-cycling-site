## Key Takeaways

The big thing Wakefield kept coming back to: cadence drops as fatigue accumulates in ultra events, and most riders haven't trained for what happens when they can't turn the pedals fast anymore. You end up grinding on torque with no strength base to support it. His fix is strength work off the bike from day one of the training block, and low-cadence, torque-based efforts on the bike throughout. Not just in a build phase. The whole time.

The other thing worth taking directly from this is the submaximal fatigue test. Three minutes at threshold to 110% of threshold, every 7 to 10 days. You fill in a short questionnaire straight after — RPE, time to exhaustion, sleep, mood, weight. Wakefield gets that data back and uses it to tell whether the training stimulus is right, whether you're fatigued and need to back off, or whether you've plateaued and something needs to change. I ran a version of this through a training block last winter and the thing that surprised me was how quickly it flagged a hole in recovery before the fatigue showed up in the longer sessions. One of our NDY members tried it across a 6-week Badlands prep block and said it was the first time he'd had an objective read on whether a hard week had actually landed. It's not complicated. The hard part is being honest when you fill in the questionnaire.

Cycling's underground economy is bigger and darker than most of us realize. We're diving into three interconnected corners of this hidden world: counterfeit carbon frames that could literally snap beneath you, contaminated supplements that could end your racing career overnight, and organized crime networks systematically stealing thousands of bikes and shipping them overseas.

## Key Takeaways

- Counterfeit carbon frames from Chinese factories have dramatically lower tensile strength than genuine frames due to improper epoxy mixing and inconsistent carbon layup — putting your safety at serious risk for a few hundred euros in savings.
- Between 15-18% of dietary supplements contain unlisted banned substances or heavy metals due to lack of FDA pre-approval and cross-contamination on production lines — and under UCI strict liability rules, it's your racing license on the line, not the manufacturer's.
- Only buy supplements with third-party certification logos (Informed Sport, NSF Certified for Sport, Cologne List) to massively reduce the risk of unknowingly ingesting banned substances.
- Over 2.4 million bikes are stolen annually in the US alone, with a less than 6% recovery rate — but registering your bike's serial number increases recovery chances to 65%, compared to 8% without it.
- Bikes with ID marking stickers have a 23% recovery rate versus 8% without, and most bike thieves are opportunistic — making your bike harder to steal causes them to move on to easier targets.
- Facebook Marketplace appears in 27% of recovered stolen bike cases, yet Facebook reportedly provides no cooperation to law enforcement investigators trying to combat organized bike theft rings.

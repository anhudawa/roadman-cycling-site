Bike fitness doesn't carry over to running the way cyclists assume it does. Jimmy Whelan, former WorldTour professional turned professional runner, came on the Roadman Cycling podcast to go through exactly where cyclists go wrong, from shoe choice to mechanics to where running fits in a training week.

## Key Takeaways

The shoe question comes first. Wheelen is specific: 40-45mm foam stack height, no carbon plate, every major brand has a version. The soft foam absorbs load that your bones and tendons aren't ready for. Carbon-plated shoes are designed for fast running with forefoot landing. If you're a cyclist jogging at 6-minute kilometer pace, you're probably landing mid-heel, and that shoe is working against you. Get the right shoe before the first run, not after the first injury.

Where cyclists actually wreck themselves is the mechanics, not the pace. Heavy heel striking with hips not rolled forward sends your full bodyweight straight down through the leg with nowhere to go. Wheelen says a pro cyclist can do two runs in the offseason and end up in trouble with the team physio. His fix before any running: plyometrics. Skipping and single-leg box jumps at the end of a gym session teach your brain and tendons how to land. Five minutes. You can do it at home. Do that for a few weeks before the first run, and your tendons arrive ready for the load instead of learning the hard way.

## Key Takeaways

The reason most cyclists skip pre-ride activation is that banded glute bridges don't make the highlights reel. Nobody's posting their monster walks on Strava. But I had a rider come to me with chronic knee pain, diagnosed by a physio as underactive glutes. Ten-minute glute activation routine, done consistently before rides, and the knee pain went. His one-minute and five-minute critical power numbers jumped too, because his glutes were finally stabilising his knee on every pedal stroke instead of letting it cave in. That's the thing about cycling training — it's quad-dominant by design, and if you just roll out the door cold, the glutes and the stabilisers never really show up. Research in other sports shows injury rates cut by up to 50% with specific warm-up routines. Five exercises is all it takes: banded glute bridge, monster walks, low lunge with a twist, dead bug core activation, leg swings. Ten minutes maximum, and your body is actually switched on before you clip in.

## 1. Encoding artifacts

### Root cause

A previous `chore: standardise all pricing to USD ($)` pass appears to
have used a byte-level substitution that replaced `\xe2` (and likely
`\x82` and `\xc2`) with `$`. Those bytes are the lead bytes of every
multi-byte UTF-8 character in the Latin/Punctuation blocks, so every
em-dash, en-dash, smart-quote, arrow, section-sign, box-drawing char,
etc. in the codebase ended up with one of its bytes turned into a
literal `$`. The result was patterns like `$��`, `$�9`, `$�$` showing
up in comments, docs, JSON, tests, and prototype READMEs.

### What was fixed

A new script at `scripts/restore-stripped-utf8.py` walks the repo and
restores known-safe corrupted byte sequences. It uses an explicit
allowlist of `(corrupted-bytes → original-bytes)` pairs so it can
never invent characters that weren't there.

Coverage of the allowlist:

- **Punctuation**: `—`, `–`, `'`, `'`, `"`, `"`, `…`, `·`, `•`
- **Arrows**: `→`, `←`, `↑`, `↓`, `↔`, `↕`
- **Symbols**: `§`, `©`, `®`, `°`, `≤`, `≥`, `√`, `⏭`, `⚠`, `✅`, `❌`, `❗`, `✓`, `✗`
- **Box drawings**: `─`, `━`, `═`, `│`, `├`, `└`, `┌`, `┐`, `┘`, `▌`, `█`, `░`
- **Currency** (intentionally restored where corrupted, since editorial
  mentions of £/€ pricing in podcast transcripts and blog posts are
  factual content): `£`, `€`, `¥`

Result: **4,774 replacements across 49 files**, including 10 in
`next.config.ts` (all in comments — no functional change).

### What's left

A handful of bytes remain in two SEO spec docs
(`docs/superpowers/specs/2026-04-09-sponsor-page-redesign.md`,
`docs/superpowers/specs/2026-04-24-ask-roadman-mvp-design.md`)
where the corruption hit two adjacent multi-byte chars in ways that
can't be unambiguously reversed without human review. Production code
and shipped pages are clean.

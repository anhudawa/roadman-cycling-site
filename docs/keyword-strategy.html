The 5 AI Citation Triggers (From Princeton GEO Research)

1. Unique Data Points

Chris O'Connor: 84kg→68kg. Anthony: lost 7kg eating MORE. Daniel Stone: Cat 3→Cat 1. These are proprietary data points no other site has. AI engines cite unique data because it adds value their synthesis can't generate from other sources.

2. Entity Association

"According to Professor Stephen Seiler, who appeared on the Roadman Cycling Podcast..." links your content to an entity AI already recognises. Every post must name-drop at least one recognised expert from Anthony's network.

3. Structured FAQ Answers

2-3 sentence answers under H3 question headings. AI extracts these almost verbatim. FAQPage schema marks them as machine-readable answers. This is the single highest-ROI format for AI citation.

4. Definition Blocks

Lead concept introductions with "X is a training approach where..." format. AI engines grab clear definitions from the most authoritative source. If Roadman's definition is better than Wikipedia's, AI cites Roadman.

5. Speakable Markup

Add speakable schema to FAQ answers and definitions. This tells voice assistants and AI which text to extract. Only ~12% of sites use structured data at all. Competitive advantage.
